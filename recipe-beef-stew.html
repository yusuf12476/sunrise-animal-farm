Hearty Farmhouse Beef Stew

A comforting, slow-cooked stew featuring tender chunks of our grass-fed beef and root vegetables. The perfect meal for a chilly evening.

Prep Time

25 mins

Cook Time

2-3 hours

Servings

6-8

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs Sunrise Farm Beef, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup dry red wine (optional)
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1.5 lbs potatoes, peeled and cubed

Instructions

  1. Prepare Beef: In a bowl, toss the beef cubes with flour, salt, and pepper until evenly coated.
  2. Brown the Beef: Heat olive oil in a large Dutch oven or heavy-bottomed pot over medium-high heat. Add the beef in batches, being careful not to overcrowd the pot. Brown on all sides. Remove the beef and set aside.
  3. Saut√© Vegetables: In the same pot, add the onion, carrots, and celery. Cook until softened, about 5-7 minutes. Add the garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  4. Deglaze and Simmer: If using, pour in the red wine to deglaze the pot, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom. Bring to a simmer and cook for a few minutes. Stir in the beef broth, tomato paste, thyme, and bay leaves.
  5. Slow Cook: Return the browned beef to the pot. Bring the stew to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and cook for 1.5 hours, stirring occasionally.
  6. Add Potatoes & Finish: Add the cubed potatoes to the stew. Cover and continue to cook for another 45-60 minutes, or until the beef and potatoes are tender. Remove bay leaves before serving.

Chef's Tip

For the best flavor, don't skip browning the beef. This step creates a deep, rich base for the stew. The stew is even better the next day as the flavors have more time to meld together.
