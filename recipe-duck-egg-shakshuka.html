Rich Duck Egg Shakshuka

A hearty, flavorful dish where rich duck eggs are gently poached in a spicy, aromatic tomato and pepper sauce. Perfect for any meal of the day.

Prep Time

15 mins

Cook Time

25-30 mins

Servings

2-4

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper (or to taste)
  • 1 (28-ounce) can crushed tomatoes
  • 4 Sunrise Farm Duck Eggs
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley, for garnish
  • Crumbled Goat Cheese (optional)
  • Crusty bread, for serving

Instructions

  1. Saut√© Aromatics: Heat olive oil in a large skillet or pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and bell pepper and cook until softened, about 5-7 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  2. Add Spices and Tomatoes: Stir in the cumin, smoked paprika, and cayenne pepper. Cook for 1 minute until the spices are aromatic. Pour in the crushed tomatoes and season with salt and pepper. Bring to a simmer and let it cook for about 10 minutes, allowing the sauce to thicken slightly.
  3. Make Wells for Eggs: Using the back of a spoon, create four small wells in the tomato sauce.
  4. Add Eggs: Carefully crack one duck egg into each well. The richness of the duck eggs will make the dish extra decadent.
  5. Cook Eggs: Reduce the heat to low, cover the skillet, and cook for 5-8 minutes, or until the egg whites are set but the yolks are still runny.
  6. Garnish and Serve: Remove from heat. Garnish generously with fresh cilantro or parsley and optional crumbled goat cheese. Serve immediately with warm, crusty bread for dipping.

Why Duck Eggs?

Duck eggs have larger, richer yolks and a higher fat content than chicken eggs, which makes this shakshuka incredibly creamy and satisfying. They are a fantastic way to elevate this already delicious dish!
