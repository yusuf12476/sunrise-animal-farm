Savory Goat Cheese and Herb Tart

A sophisticated yet simple tart that showcases the creamy tang of fresh goat cheese and the bright flavors of garden herbs.

Prep Time

20 mins

Cook Time

35-40 mins

Servings

6-8

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3-4 tablespoons ice water

For the Filling:

  • 8 oz Sunrise Farm Goat Cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 large Sunrise Farm Fresh Egg
  • 2 tbsp fresh chives, chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • Optional: Drizzle of Sunrise Farm Raw Honey

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Crust: In a food processor, pulse flour, salt, and cold butter until it resembles coarse crumbs. Add ice water, 1 tablespoon at a time, pulsing until the dough just comes together. Form into a disk, wrap in plastic, and chill for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Pre-bake the Crust: Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Roll out the dough and fit it into a 9-inch tart pan with a removable bottom. Prick the bottom with a fork, line with parchment paper, and fill with pie weights. Bake for 15 minutes. Remove weights and parchment, then bake for another 5-7 minutes until lightly golden. Let cool slightly.
  3. Make the Filling: In a medium bowl, whisk together the softened goat cheese, heavy cream, and egg until smooth. Stir in the fresh chives and thyme. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Assemble and Bake: Pour the filling into the pre-baked crust and spread evenly. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the filling is set and the top is lightly golden.
  5. Serve: Let the tart cool in the pan for about 10 minutes before removing the outer ring. Garnish with more fresh herbs and a drizzle of honey, if desired. Serve warm or at room temperature.

Tips & Variations

For extra flavor, add some caramelized onions or sautéed mushrooms to the bottom of the crust before pouring in the filling. You can also experiment with different herbs like rosemary or dill.
